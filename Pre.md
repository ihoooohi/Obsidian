## ## 1. Planning

“After contouring, the next step is Planning.

In this stage, a treatment plan is created.  
This plan decides how the radiation will be given.

The goal is simple:

- Give enough radiation to treat the tumor，tumor is the bad part of a organ
    
- Protect the healthy parts of the body as much as possible
    
Summarily, this step need to choose which part need to be given radiation, and which part need to be protected

---

## 2. Delivery

“The next step is Delivery, which is the actual treatment.

The patient lies on a treatment table in the same position used during the CT scan.

Before each session, images are taken to make sure the body is in the correct position. This is important because even small movements can affect accuracy.

Each session only lasts a few minutes, and the patient does not feel the radiation during the treatment.”

---

## 3. Follow-up

“The final step is Follow-up.

After the treatment is completed, the patient returns for regular check-ups.

Doctors check:

- Whether the tumor has responded to the treatment
    
- Whether there are any side effects
    
- Whether recovery is going well
    

Follow-up is important because it helps evaluate the success of the treatment and ensures the patient’s long-term health.”
